Crystal Marie Moore - Copy Strategy

type: copy-strategy status: complete prospect: crystal-moore verified: 2026-05-16

Voice Register

Primary: Executive-formal, female-founder, broker-owner, Caribbean-American operator.

She is not a hustle-culture influencer. She is not a content creator. She is a licensed broker who runs her own firm, holds advanced designations (CRB, SFR, MRP), and has personally closed over $100M in transactions. Her register is polished, confident, and transactional in the best sense: clear about value, clear about outcomes.

Modifiers: - Female founder energy: builds trust through competence, not charisma. Does not need to be the loudest voice in the room. - Caribbean-American: warmth under the formal exterior. One-on-one care is a real differentiator for her (visible in testimonials: "one on one personal care to a level not commonly found in today's market"). - Broker-owner: talks about the brokerage as her creation, not just her employer. Pride of ownership is in the copy.

Tokens Extracted from Her Surfaces (dominionmoves.com)

Her copy vocabulary: - "Your Vision Meets Our Excellence" (primary tagline) - "Experience Excellence" (secondary) - "Over $100M in closed sales and more than 1,000 clients served" (proof anchor) - "husband and wife team that brings professionalism and one on one personal care" (from testimonial copy she chose to feature) - "thoroughly prepared with area comps, projections, and a complete delineation of all the marketing methods" (language from client testimonial she chose to feature -- signals she values preparation and thoroughness) - "Join South Florida's Top Boutique Brokerage" (B2B recruitment angle) - "100% Commission Plans Available" (agent recruitment value prop)

Register signals: - "Excellence" appears twice (tagline + secondary CTA) -- she uses this word deliberately - "Boutique" brokerage framing -- positions against large chain brokerages on intimacy and quality - "Prepared" and "professional" appear in the testimonials she chose to feature -- she curates proof around competence, not personality - "Vision" in the tagline -- aspirational, client-centered, not self-centered

Tone Rules for Crystal-Voice Copy

DO: - Lead with outcomes, not processes ("closed", "served", "referred", "listened") - Use formal contractions sparingly ("we're" is fine, but "they're gonna" is not) - Reference credentials and designations when relevant (CRB, SFR, MRP) - Keep sentences clean and direct. No winding subordinate clauses. - Acknowledge the market reality (insurance, condo rules, shifting conditions) without catastrophizing - "Your" not "one's" -- she speaks to the specific client, not generically - Warmth is earned through specificity, not exclamation points

DO NOT: - Em-dashes (never) - "Unlock", "elevate your", "next-level", "game-changer", "seamlessly", "effortlessly" - Hipster lowercase IG aesthetic -- she is executive-formal - Self-deprecating or imposter-syndrome language - Passive voice when active is available - Vague filler: "amazing results", "incredible journey" - Real estate clichés: "dream home", "keys to your future", "turn the key on a new chapter"

Andrei-to-Crystal Voice Bridge

When writing outreach FROM Andrei TO Crystal, keep Andrei's direct, confident, numbers-led register. Do not over-formalize. Crystal is an entrepreneur. She respects directness.

When writing Crystal-voice copy (her landing page, her IG content), shift fully into her register. The outreach and her own content have different voices.

Framing Priorities

  1. The 12-month gap reframe: Not abandonment. Not forgetfulness. More research done, sharper plan.
  2. Funnel-not-marketing framing: Do not pitch "marketing." Pitch infrastructure: "the capture layer," "the follow-up system," "the agent recruitment funnel." She is a builder. She responds to infrastructure language.
  3. She is already winning: Do not position this as "your business is broken." Position it as "you have the credibility; the digital layer is not matching it yet."
  4. RE compliance awareness: She is a licensed broker in Florida (FS 475). All copy for her surfaces stays within FREC/NAR advertising standards. No performance guarantees, no misleading superiority claims. The footer disclaimer on the landing page covers transaction disclosures.
