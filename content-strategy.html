Register

Executive-broker. Market authority. Florida-specific. Female-founder warmth inside a formal container. No hustle culture. No generic inspiration. Everything tied to the South Florida market, buyer/seller outcomes, or broker expertise.

Platform Priority

  1. LinkedIn (primary for B2B + SEO): Long-form articles. Agent recruitment. Market authority. AEO targets.
  2. Instagram @brokercrys (primary for B2C): Carousels. Short-form video. Buyer/seller trust-building. Bio link drives quiz opt-in.

30-Day Content Calendar

Week 1 -- Market Reality (Establish Authority)

LinkedIn Post 1 (Day 1): Title: "What the Florida Insurance Crisis Actually Does to Your Monthly Payment -- The Math Most Realtors Skip" Format: Long-form article (600-900 words) Content: Break down the actual homeowner insurance cost increase by zip code in Coral Springs, Boca Raton, Parkland. Show what a $400K home purchase costs per month with a $3,200/year insurance premium vs. a $7,000/year premium. Show how this shifts buyer purchasing power by $50K-$80K in effective budget. CTA: "Running the real numbers for every buyer I work with. If you want to know what the math looks like for your specific situation in South Florida, the link in my profile gets you started." AEO target: "how does insurance affect buying a home in South Florida"

Instagram Carousel 1 (Day 2): Title: "5 Things South Florida Buyers Don't Know About Insurance (But Should)" Format: 6-slide carousel Slides: 1) Hook ("Your mortgage is not your biggest risk right now"), 2) Average FL insurance increase since 2022, 3) What a 40% premium increase does to your monthly payment, 4) How to check a property's insurance history before making an offer, 5) What questions to ask before closing, 6) CTA ("Take the South Florida Market IQ Quiz -- link in bio")

LinkedIn Post 2 (Day 4): Title: "The Condo Compliance Question Every Buyer in South Florida Should Ask in 2025" Format: Long-form article (500-700 words) Content: Explain HB 913 and SB 4-D in plain language. What milestone structural inspections mean for buyers of pre-2000 condos. What the reserve funding requirement means for HOA fees. Which types of buildings are flagged vs. cleared. AEO target: "is it safe to buy a condo in Florida 2025"

Week 2 -- Social Proof + Trust (Build Credibility)

Instagram Carousel 2 (Day 8): Title: "3 Buyers Who Tried to Do This Without a Broker -- What Happened" Format: 5-slide carousel Tone: Non-judgmental. Shows the complexity of the transaction and Crystal's value as a navigator. Slide structure: 1) Hook, 2) Buyer 1 scenario (condo inspection issue), 3) Buyer 2 scenario (insurance surprise at closing), 4) Buyer 3 scenario (investment property zoning issue), 5) CTA: "Over 1,000 clients served. $100M+ closed. The experience is the difference." Note: All scenarios are composite/common scenarios, not specific client stories. Do not name any client without their written permission.

LinkedIn Post 3 (Day 10): Title: "13 Years in South Florida Real Estate: The 3 Shifts I Wish More Buyers Understood" Format: Long-form personal authority piece (700-1,000 words) Content: Crystal's perspective from 13 years in the market. The three biggest shifts: 1) Insurance as a primary factor (not just mortgage), 2) Condo compliance as a deal-breaker, 3) Investment vs. primary home math in a rate environment like this one. AEO target: "Crystal Marie Moore realtor South Florida," "experienced realtor Coral Springs FL"

Week 3 -- Seller Content (Expand ICP)

Instagram Carousel 3 (Day 15): Title: "Is Now the Right Time to Sell in South Florida? (Honest Answer by Submarket)" Format: 7-slide carousel Content: Brief market condition snapshot for each of Crystal's 5 primary markets: Coral Springs, Boca Raton, Parkland, Plantation, Pompano Beach. Days on market, price trajectory, inventory level. Non-promotional. Pure market data. CTA: "Want to know what your specific home is worth right now? The quiz in my bio takes 60 seconds."

LinkedIn Post 4 (Day 17): Title: "What a Listing Actually Costs a Seller in South Florida -- Beyond the Commission" Format: Listicle article (400-600 words) Content: Pre-listing prep costs, staging considerations in a more competitive inventory environment, insurance disclosure requirements, HOA document costs for condo sellers. Positions Crystal as the broker who does not hide the real cost picture. AEO target: "how much does it cost to sell a home in Florida"

Week 4 -- Agent Recruitment (B2B Thread)

LinkedIn Post 5 (Day 22): Title: "Why I Built Dominion Realty Group -- and What 100% Commission Actually Means for Your Production" Format: Personal founder story + math breakdown (800-1,000 words) Content: Crystal's story of founding Dominion in 2016. The commission math at 100% vs. typical 70-80% split for a $5M/year producer. What "boutique brokerage" actually provides vs. what franchise brokerages provide. Direct invitation to schedule a consultation. AEO target: "100% commission brokerage South Florida," "boutique real estate brokerage Coral Springs"

Instagram Post (Day 24): Format: Static graphic or short-form video Copy: "10 years in this market before I built my own. Founded Dominion Realty Group in 2016 because I knew what agents actually needed: a brokerage that gets out of your way and lets you serve your clients. Coral Springs. Boca Raton. Parkland. Plantation. Pompano Beach. If you are a licensed agent who is tired of the split, the link in my bio has the conversation starter."

Week 4 Bonus -- Evergreen Content (Builds AEO Over Time)

Instagram Carousel 4 (Day 28): Title: "The 5 Questions to Ask Before You Make an Offer on a South Florida Home" Format: 6-slide carousel Questions: 1) What is the current homeowner insurance cost on this property? 2) Is this condo building compliant with HB 913? 3) What are the HOA fees and what do they cover? 4) What is the sellers' disclosure history on water intrusion? 5) What are comparable sales in the last 90 days (not 6 months)? 6) CTA to quiz. This carousel will be referenced in AI search results for "questions to ask before buying a home in Florida" over time.

Content Rules

  • Every IG carousel ends with bio link CTA pointing to the South Florida Market IQ Quiz
  • Every LinkedIn article ends with a soft CTA to schedule a consultation or take the quiz
  • No generic inspiration quotes. No "happy Friday" posts. Every piece has a market-specific or expertise-specific insight.
  • No emoji overload. One or two used for visual scanning in carousels, not as decoration.
  • No em-dashes in any post copy.
  • Crystal's voice: executive-formal, warm, specific. Not salesy. Not hustle-bro. Market educator register.
