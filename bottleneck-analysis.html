Crystal Marie Moore - Bottleneck Analysis

type: bottleneck-analysis status: complete prospect: crystal-moore verified: 2026-05-16 tier: warm-reentry audience-tier: sub-30K (9,434 IG followers) y1-floor-per-bottleneck: $15K-$60K

Audience tier: sub-30K. Y1 floor per bottleneck: $15K-$60K. Ceiling rarely above $300K/year from a single digital funnel at this tier.

Bottleneck 1: No Email Capture on 9,400+ Follower Audience

Snapshot: Crystal has 9,434 IG followers and $100M+ in closed volume. Zero visible lead magnet, quiz, or email opt-in anywhere on her public surfaces. Someone who sees a reel, taps the bio, hits dominionmoves.com -- and is not ready to call right now -- has nowhere soft to land. They leave.

Asymmetry: The follower base represents real buyer/seller/investor intent. Florida RE market shift (insurance crisis, condo compliance) means this audience is actively researching. Content does the trust-building work. There is no mechanism to capture that trust before it evaporates.

Cost of Waiting: Every month without an email capture is 300-600 content-warm visitors who see the content, want the expertise, and exit with no follow-up path. 12 months of that = 3,600-7,200 cold exits. At 1-2% conversion on a warmed email list, that is 36-144 buyer/seller inquiries never had. At average RE commission in South Florida ($8,000-$18,000), cost is $288K-$2.6M in missed pipeline over 12 months. Even 10% of that number is material.

Fix: A "Florida Buyer IQ Quiz" or "South Florida Market Pulse" calculator captures name + email + buyer/seller intent. Segments the list by timeline (buying now / buying in 6-12 months / selling / investing). Routes warm leads to booking, cold leads to a 5-email nurture.

Y1 Floor: $15K-$35K incremental from email-driven referral reactivation and inquiry capture. Compounds every year the list grows.

Bottleneck 2: No Digital Nurture Sequence (Single-Channel Intake)

Snapshot: The only path to Crystal today is: see her on IG, go to dominionmoves.com, call or fill out a contact form. There is no email sequence. No SMS follow-up. No pre-call education that arrives before the consultation. No post-consult follow-up drip. Single-channel intake on a broker who closes $100M+ is a structural fragility -- one algorithm change or one slow content month and the pipeline stalls.

Asymmetry: Crystal is a credentialed expert (CRB, SFR, MRP) with 13+ years of market knowledge. That expertise is not packaged into any automated touchpoint that runs while she sleeps. The competition -- Redfin, Zillow-affiliated teams, RE/MAX -- has full CRM drip infrastructure running 24/7. Crystal competes on relationship; she is not compounding the relationship digitally.

Cost of Waiting: Each buyer or seller who reaches out, gets a manual follow-up or none, and goes cold is a $8K-$18K commission left in the pipeline. At a 10% lead-to-close rate, every 10 stale leads = 1 missed closing. If she runs 50 consultations/year and 20% go cold after first contact with no follow-up drip, that is 10 missed closings = $80K-$180K/year in commissions that a nurture sequence would have recovered.

Fix: A 5-7 email post-inquiry sequence (day 1, 3, 7, 14, 30). Content: South Florida market insight, insurance math explainer, buyer pre-qualification checklist, investor ROI framework. Positions Crystal as the educator before the transaction. Ends with a calendar link. Runs on autopilot in GHL or ConvertKit.

Y1 Floor: $20K-$60K in recovered pipeline from leads that would otherwise go cold. Conservative on this tier.

Bottleneck 3: Dominion Agent Recruitment Has No Funnel

Snapshot: dominionmoves.com advertises "Join South Florida's Top Boutique Brokerage -- 100% Commission Plans Available." There is a Calendly link. That is the entire recruitment funnel. No landing page optimized for agent acquisition. No VSL from Crystal explaining the culture and the split structure. No email sequence to recruit agents who are considering a switch. No case studies from current Dominion agents.

Asymmetry: Agent recruitment is a B2B sale with a longer sales cycle than a consumer RE transaction. A one-line CTA and a Calendly link is not a B2B conversion path. The agents Crystal wants to recruit are comparing Dominion against brokerages with full pitch decks, culture videos, and onboarding materials. The information gap makes Crystal's 100% commission plan look unsubstantiated.

Cost of Waiting: Each agent who joins Dominion represents desk fees and/or a split on every deal they close. One productive agent closing $3M-$5M/year = $15K-$25K/year in brokerage revenue (transaction fees, E&O, tech fees, split retention). Every month with no agent funnel = zero systematic recruitment activity. Over 12 months with even 2 agents who could have been recruited = $30K-$50K in recurring brokerage revenue foregone.

Fix: A dedicated agent-recruitment landing page with Crystal's VSL ("Why I Built Dominion and What 100% Commission Actually Means"), a 3-email agent-nurture sequence (sent after Calendly booking: Day 0 confirmation, Day 2 culture doc, Day 5 onboarding FAQ), and a simple agent testimonial block.

Y1 Floor: $15K-$25K in new brokerage revenue from 1-2 additional recruited agents. Multiplies year over year.

Bottleneck 4: Two-Domain Brand Fragmentation

Snapshot: crystalmariemoore.com is the personal brand. dominionmoves.com is the brokerage. These are separate domains with separate Google Business Profiles (if any), separate SEO footprints, and likely different design languages. A buyer who Googles "Crystal Marie Moore realtor Coral Springs" may land on either, or neither. Authority signals are split. Backlinks are split.

Asymmetry: Florida RE search queries in Coral Springs / Boca Raton / Parkland / Plantation / Pompano Beach are high-value. "Realtor Coral Springs FL," "luxury homes Boca Raton," "investment properties Parkland" are buyer-intent terms. A unified SEO strategy on one domain authority scores higher than two thin domains competing. Crystal's 13 years of expertise, $100M+ volume, and CRB credential are not surfacing in organic search because authority is diluted.

Cost of Waiting: Organic search in South Florida RE is a high-stakes channel. Zillow and Realtor.com dominate page 1. But "Crystal Marie Moore realtor" and "Dominion Realty Group Coral Springs" queries should be fully owned by her. Without a unified content strategy, those branded queries leak to Zillow profiles. Estimated 12-month SEO revenue at risk: 10-20 organic buyer/seller leads/year who could have been captured = $80K-$360K in commissions depending on close rate.

Fix: Content strategy recommendation: establish dominionmoves.com as the SEO anchor (brokerage legitimacy + scale signals). Point crystalmariemoore.com to it or use it as personal-brand interlink. Publish 2x/month on Florida insurance crisis, condo regulations, Coral Springs market data, Boca Raton luxury investment. Targets AEO (AI-generated answer position) and traditional SERPs simultaneously.

Y1 Floor: $15K-$40K from 2-4 organic leads converted through content-driven authority. Baseline conservative for year 1.

Bottleneck 5: Florida Market Shift = Positioning Vacuum Crystal Has Not Filled

Snapshot: The Florida RE market is in a documented transition: insurance premium spikes (some areas up 40-60% since 2022), condo legislation creating buyer hesitation on pre-HB-913 buildings, inventory shifting toward buyer-favorable terms in certain submarkets. This is top-of-mind for every buyer and seller in Crystal's market. No observable Crystal Moore content is addressing it directly.

Asymmetry: The brokers who own this conversation -- "what do the insurance changes mean for my home value?" "which condos are safe to buy right now?" "is now the right time to sell in Parkland?" -- capture the organic research moment before the buyer or seller ever picks up a phone. Crystal has the credentials (CRB, SFR, 13 years South FL market experience) to be the authoritative voice on this. She is not publishing it.

Cost of Waiting: Every competitor who publishes a "2025 Florida Condo Buyer's Guide" or "Insurance Math for South Florida Homebuyers" before Crystal does, owns that search intent. Once a competitor ranks and gets cited by AI search engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews), dislodging them costs 12-24 months of catch-up content.

Fix: A "Florida Market Intelligence" content series (4-8 LinkedIn articles + IG carousel posts): insurance math explainer, condo compliance buyer's checklist, Coral Springs vs. Parkland investment comparison, 2025 South Florida market outlook. Positions Crystal as the resident expert, not just a transactional agent. Feeds into the email capture (Bottleneck 1) and the nurture sequence (Bottleneck 2).

Y1 Floor: $15K-$30K from content-driven inbound. Builds long-term. Compounds with every published piece.
